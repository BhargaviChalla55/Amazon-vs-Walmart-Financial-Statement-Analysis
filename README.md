# Amazon-vs-Walmart-Financial-Statement-Analysis
**Project Overview**:

This project presents a comprehensive financial statement and valuation analysis of **Amazon.com, Inc.,** with **Walmart Inc.** as the primary competitor.
The analysis follows professional financial analyst guidelines, focusing on:

 - Profitability
 - Risk
 - Operating efficiency
 - Liquidity & solvency
 - Firm valuation

**Objective**:

To evaluate Amazon’s financial performance and risk profile from an **investor’s perspective** and provide a **stock recommendation** based on:
- Financial ratios
- Operating analysis
- Valuation modeling

**Data Sources**

- **WRDS** (Wharton Research Data Services) – Financial statements, stock data, and firm-level information
- SEC 10-K Reports
- Company Annual Reports
- SEC **DEF 14A** (Proxy Statements) – Executive compensation and governance details.

**Scope of Analysis**

- Company background and business strategy
- Auditor and management reports
- Executive compensation analysis
- Profitability, liquidity, solvency, and coverage ratios
- DuPont and RNOA disaggregation
- Productivity and cash conversion cycle analysis
- Credit risk assessment using Z-Score
- Firm valuation using Discounted Cash Flow (DCF)
- Final investment recommendation

**Tools & Methods**

- Microsoft Excel (financial modeling & calculations)
- Financial Ratio Analysis
- DuPont & RNOA Models
- Z-Score Credit Risk Model
- Discounted Cash Flow (DCF) Valuation

**Key Findings**

- Amazon’s profitability growth is primarily **margin-driven** (AWS and Advertising).
- Walmart’s performance is driven by **asset efficiency and scale**.
- Amazon’s financial leverage has **decreased significantly** in recent years.
- DCF valuation suggests Amazon’s stock may be **overvalued** under conservative assumptions.

**Files Included**

- PDF Report – Full financial statement analysis report.
- Excel File – Ratio calculations, assumptions, and valuation model
